A Plan for the Upper Genesee River: Genesee River Wilds Project, 2nd Edition

Genesee River Watershed and Genesee River Wilds Target Area, with Riverside Multi-Use Trails


Genesee River Wilds River Access Site (via Caneadea Creek), Caneadea, NY

The Genesee River Wilds Plan is the second major edition of a watershed plan for the upper Genesee River from the riverâ€™s sources in Potter County, Pennsylvania, to the southern boundary of Letchworth State Park in New York State. The rudimentary first edition released in 2008 was called "Genesee River Wilds Project." The incremental updates since that time have finally demanded a complete overhaul. The new name presented in the 2016 edition represents the greater maturity of the plan's design, support, and implementation.

The mission of the second edition remains identical with that of the first: To restore, protect, and enjoy the upper Genesee River by combining conservation, recreation, and business. The strategies by which this mission is being fulfilled also remain identical with those of the first edition: Develop (1) a corridor of forested riparian (streamside) buffers along the upper Genesee River and its tributaries that include (2) a system of minimally-invasive riverside nature parks connected by (3) a multi-use trail (rail-trail for bicycles, hiking, and other active transportation) and (4) a blueway (water trail for canoes and kayaks).

The most exciting difference between the editions of 2008 and 2016 is the contrast between their contexts. The intervening years have witnessed dramatic progress on the plan's implementation. The magnitude of the work is still daunting and often overwhelming. But we now have enough new infrastructure that an increasingly large number of people are sharing in the "enjoy" part of our mission. This is having the intended effect of encouraging them to help us "restore and protect" the Genesee River. We invite you to join.
