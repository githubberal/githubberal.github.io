A Plan for the Upper Genesee River

Genesee River Watershed and Genesee River Wilds Target Area, with Riverside Multi-Use Trails


Genesee River Wilds River Access Site (via Caneadea Creek), Caneadea, NY

A New 2016 Edition of the Genesee River Wilds Project Plan from 2008: The Genesee River Wilds Plan of 2016 is the second major edition of a watershed plan for the upper Genesee River from the riverâ€™s sources in Potter County, Pennsylvania, to the southern boundary of Letchworth State Park in New York State. The rudimentary first edition released in 2008 was called "The Genesee River Wilds Project." This was updated incrementally, but a complete overhaul has been long overdue. The new rubric of this 2016 edition represents the greater maturity of the plan's design, support, and implementation. The plan's mission and the strategies by which the mission is being achieved remain the same.

Mission: To restore, protect, and enjoy the upper Genesee River by combining conservation, recreation, and business.

Strategy: Develop (1) a corridor of forested riparian (streamside) buffers along the upper Genesee River and its tributaries that include (2) a system of minimally-invasive riverside nature parks connected by (3) a greenway with a multi-use trail (rail-trail for bicycles, hiking, and other active transportation) and (4) a blueway (water trail for canoes and kayaks). Wherever realistically possible, this forested riparian corridor will reach at least 300 feet wide on each side of the main stem of the Genesee River and at least 100 feet on each side of its major tributaries.

Rationale: Nature parks, trails, and the blueway will provide a toehold for expanding the acreage of forested riparian buffers. They also will generate the necessary community support by providing infrastructure for eco-tourism and other sustainable business. Further incentives for river conservation will come from other benefits of this new recreational system. These include new amenities for health, quality of life, outdoor education, and making communities more appealing to educated young professionals and graduates from area universities who otherwise would take their job-creating abilities to other areas where such amenties are already available.

Major difference from 2008 edition: The most dramatic difference between the editions of 2008 and 2016 is the contrast between their contexts. The intervening years have witnessed exciting progress on the plan's implementation. Sometimes this success has made the magnitude of the work even more overwhelming. But we now have enough new infrastructure that an increasingly large number of people are sharing in the "enjoy" part of our mission. This is having the intended effect of encouraging them to help us "restore and protect" the Genesee River. We invite you to join.
