A Plan for the Upper Genesee River, 2nd editon, 2016

Contacts

The Genesee River Wilds Plan is the second edition of a watershed plan for the upper Genesee River from the riverâ€™s sources in Potter County, Pennsylvania, to the southern boundary of Letchworth State Park in New York State. The rudimentary first edition released in 2008 was called "Genesee River Wilds Project." The new name represents the greater maturity of the plan's design, support, and implementation. The mission and associated strategy of the second edition remains identical with that of the first edition: To restore, protect, and enjoy the upper Genesee River by combining conservation, recreation, and business. The specific goals by which this mission is being fulfilled also remain identical with those of the first edition: Develop (1) a corridor of forested riparian (streamside) buffers along the upper Genesee River and its tributaries that include (2) a system of minimally-invasive riverside nature parks connected by (3) a multi-use trail (rail-trail for bicycles, hiking, and other active transportation) and (4) a blueway (water trail for canoes and kayaks). The most exciting difference between the editions of 2008 and 2016 is the contrast between their contexts. The first edition expressed what to many seemed an overly ambitious vision. Its grand scope was informed by the recognition that previous conservation efforts were far too limited in scale to reverse the trajectory of relative neglect of the upper Genesee River or provide sufficient defenses against rapidly growing threats. The intervening years have witnessed dramatic progress on the plan's implementation. The magnitude of the work is still daunting and often overwhelming. But we have made enough progress that many people already benefit from an abundance of new infrastructure for the "enjoy" part of our mission. We invite you to join us.
